## Content Strategy Framework

### The 5 Questions
1. **Why** - What business goal does this content serve?
2. **Who** - Who is this content for?
3. **What** - What type of content will we create?
4. **Where** - Where will it live and be distributed?
5. **When** - What's the cadence and timeline?

## Content Pillars

### What Are Pillars?
3-5 core topics that all content ties back to. They should:
- Align with business goals
- Match audience interests
- Differentiate from competitors
- Be sustainable long-term

### Example Pillar Structure
```
Brand: Project Management SaaS

Pillar 1: Productivity
- Time management tips
- Focus and deep work
- Tool optimization

Pillar 2: Team Collaboration
- Communication best practices
- Remote work strategies
- Meeting effectiveness

Pillar 3: Project Planning
- Planning frameworks
- Resource management
- Deadline management

Pillar 4: Leadership
- Managing teams
- Career growth
- Industry trends
```

### Content Ratio
Typical distribution:
- Educational/value content: 60-70%
- Promotional content: 10-20%
- Engagement/community content: 10-20%

## Content Performance

### Metrics by Goal
| Goal | Metrics |
|------|---------|
| Awareness | Traffic, impressions, reach, rankings |
| Engagement | Time on page, comments, shares, saves |
| Conversion | Leads, sign-ups, downloads, sales |
| Retention | Return visits, engagement rate, NPS |

### Content Scoring
Rate each piece on:
- Performance (traffic, engagement)
- Relevance (still accurate?)
- Quality (meets current standards?)
- Alignment (serves business goals?)

## Content Repurposing

### One-to-Many Model
```
Pillar Content (long-form guide)
├── 5-10 Blog posts (sections expanded)
├── 20+ Social posts (key points)
├── 1 Email series (sequential)
├── 1 Webinar (interactive version)
├── 1 Video (visual version)
└── 1 Infographic (visual summary)
```

### Repurposing Matrix

## Content Governance

### Content Standards
- Voice and tone guidelines
- Quality benchmarks
- SEO requirements
- Approval workflow
- Update schedule

### Content Maintenance
- Review schedule (quarterly/annually)
- Update triggers (product changes, data changes)
- Sunset criteria (when to remove)
- Redirect strategy (for removed content)

## Content Workflow

### Creation Process
```
1. Ideation
   - Brainstorm topics
   - Check against pillars
   - Validate demand (search volume, audience interest)

2. Planning
   - Create brief
   - Assign owner
   - Set deadline

3. Creation
   - Write/produce
   - Internal review
   - Revisions

4. Optimization
   - SEO optimization
   - Visual assets
   - Formatting

5. Review
   - Final approval
   - Legal/compliance (if needed)

6. Publication
   - Publish to platform
   - Distribution (email, social)

7. Promotion
   - Paid promotion (if applicable)
   - Outreach

8. Analysis
   - Track performance
   - Document learnings
```
