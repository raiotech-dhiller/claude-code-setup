# Discovery Templates

## Pre-Discovery Questionnaire

Send this questionnaire 2-3 days before the discovery call to gather baseline information and make the call more productive.

### Business Foundation

```markdown
## About Your Business

1. **Company name and website:**

2. **What does your company do?** (2-3 sentences)

3. **How long have you been in business?**

4. **Company size:**
   - [ ] Solo founder
   - [ ] 2-10 employees
   - [ ] 11-50 employees
   - [ ] 51-200 employees
   - [ ] 200+ employees

5. **Who are your ideal customers?** (Be specific: demographics, job titles, industries)

6. **What problem do you solve for them?**

7. **Who are your top 3 competitors?**
   - Competitor 1:
   - Competitor 2:
   - Competitor 3:

8. **What makes you different from them?**
```

### Project Context

```markdown
## About This Project

9. **What do you want to build?** (web app, mobile app, website, etc.)

10. **Why are you building this now?** What's driving the urgency?

11. **What does success look like?** How will you measure it?

12. **What's your target launch date?**

13. **Is this a new product or improving something existing?**
    - [ ] Building from scratch
    - [ ] Redesigning existing product
    - [ ] Adding features to existing product
    - [ ] Other: ___________

14. **If existing, please share links:**
    - Current website/app:
    - Admin/dashboard (if applicable):
```

```markdown

    - [ ] None yet

18. **Compliance/regulatory requirements?**
    - [ ] None
    - [ ] GDPR
    - [ ] HIPAA
```

## Discovery Call Script

### Opening (5 minutes)

```markdown
**Goals for this call:**
1. Understand your business and project deeply
2. Identify if we're a good fit to help
3. Answer your questions about our process

**Housekeeping:**
- Call will be ~60 minutes
- I'll be taking notes
- Feel free to interrupt with questions

**Let's start with you telling me a bit about yourself and [company]...**
```

### Business Deep Dive (15 minutes)

```markdown
**About the Business:**
- Walk me through what [company] does
- Who is your ideal customer?
- How do they find you today?
- What's your competitive advantage?

**Probing questions:**
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Can you give me an example?"
- "How does that work currently?"
```

### Project Deep Dive (20 minutes)

```markdown
**The Vision:**
- Describe the project you have in mind
- Walk me through how a user would use this
- What's the most important thing it needs to do?
- What would make this a success?

**User Journeys:**
- Who are the different types of users?
- What's the first thing they'll do?
- What's the most common action?
- What's the highest-value action?

**Current State:**
- What exists today?
- What's working well?
- What's not working?
- What have you tried before?
```

### Constraints & Priorities (10 minutes)

```markdown
**Timeline:**
- When do you need this live?
- What's driving that date?
- Any external deadlines? (launch, funding, etc.)

**Budget:**
- What budget range have you allocated?
- Is this flexible if scope changes?
- How is this funded? (bootstrapped, funded, client budget)

**Resources:**
- Who's the decision maker?
- Who else needs to be involved?
- What assets do you have ready? (design, content, branding)
```

### Our Approach (5 minutes)

```markdown
**How we work:**
- Agile sprints, typically 2 weeks
- Weekly status updates
- Dedicated Slack channel
- Collaborative tools (Figma, Linear)

**What to expect:**
- Proposal within [X] days
- Kickoff call to align on details
- Regular check-ins and demos
```

### Questions & Close (5 minutes)

```markdown
**Their questions:**
- What questions do you have for me?
- Anything we didn't cover?

**Next steps:**
- I'll send a follow-up email today
- Proposal by [date]
- Let me know if you need anything else
```

## Stakeholder Interview Guide

When multiple stakeholders are involved, conduct separate interviews to understand different perspectives.

### Executive Stakeholder

```markdown
**Focus:** Vision, business goals, success metrics

Questions:
1. What business problem does this solve?
2. How does this fit into company strategy?
3. What does success look like in 6 months? 1 year?
4. What's the risk of not doing this?
5. What budget has been allocated?
```

### Product/Marketing Stakeholder

```markdown
**Focus:** User needs, market positioning, features

Questions:
1. Who is the target user?
2. What user research have you done?
3. What are the must-have features?
4. How will this be marketed/launched?
5. What metrics will you track?
```

### Technical Stakeholder

```markdown
**Focus:** Constraints, integrations, architecture

Questions:
1. What's the current tech stack?
2. Any legacy systems to integrate?
3. What are the performance requirements?
4. Security/compliance requirements?
5. Who will maintain this long-term?
```

### End User Interviews

```markdown
**Focus:** Actual needs, pain points, workflows

Questions:
1. Walk me through your typical day
2. What's the hardest part of [task]?
3. What tools do you currently use?
4. What have you tried that didn't work?
5. If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?
```
